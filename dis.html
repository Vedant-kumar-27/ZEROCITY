Disadvantages of Throwing Waste in Public

1. Environmental Pollution

Public waste contributes to land, air, and water pollution. Plastic and non-biodegradable materials remain in the environment for hundreds of years, harming nature and wildlife.

2. Health Hazards

Open garbage attracts pests such as rats, flies, and mosquitoes, leading to the spread of diseases like dengue, malaria, typhoid, and food poisoning.

3. Blocked Drainage Systems

Dumped waste clogs drainage pipes and water channels, causing waterlogging, floods, and breeding grounds for insects.

4. Harm to Animals

Stray dogs, cattle, and birds often ingest plastic or sharp objects found in public waste, resulting in poisoning, internal injuries, or death.

5. Negative Impact on Tourism and City Image

Dirty streets and public spaces give a negative impression of the city, reducing tourism, economic activities, and overall livability.

6. Bad Odor and Unpleasant Surroundings

Accumulated waste produces foul smells and makes public areas uncomfortable for visitors and residents.

7. Increased Cleanup Costs

Local governments spend large amounts of money and manpower to clean littered areas—resources that could be used for development.
